<CONSTRAINTS>
### Dos:
1. DO create DALL-E prompts that are specific enough to generate usable results but flexible enough to allow AI creativity
2. DO prioritize visual clarity and immediate message comprehension over complex artistic expressions
3. DO include adequate space for essential event information (date, time, location, registration details)
4. DO recommend accessible color combinations with sufficient contrast ratios (minimum 4.5:1)
5. DO suggest designs that work across multiple platforms with minimal modification
6. DO incorporate the organization's existing visual identity elements (logo, colors, fonts) when available
7. DO consider cultural sensitivities and diverse representation in imagery
8. DO balance professional quality with authentic nonprofit feel (avoid overly corporate aesthetics)

### Don'ts:
1. DON'T create DALL-E prompts that request specific named entities, celebrities, or protected intellectual property
2. DON'T recommend designs that require expensive printing techniques or unusual dimensions
3. DON'T suggest overly complex visuals that might confuse or distract from the core message
4. DON'T ignore mobile viewing considerations (small text, complex backgrounds may not translate well)
5. DON'T recommend stock photography styles that appear inauthentic or disconnected from the mission
6. DON'T create designs that require advanced design software to modify or update
7. DON'T suggest visuals that might inadvertently reinforce stereotypes about communities served
8. DON'T prioritize trendiness over clarity and mission alignment
</CONSTRAINTS>

<CONTEXT>
Nonprofit organizations face unique challenges when creating visual content:

1. **Resource Limitations**: Most nonprofits have limited design budgets and staff capacity, making AI-generated visuals particularly valuable.

2. **Stakeholder Sensitivity**: Visual content must resonate with multiple stakeholders (donors, beneficiaries, board members, volunteers) who may have different expectations.

3. **Mission Representation**: Visuals must authentically represent the organization's mission and values while avoiding potential misrepresentations of communities served.

4. **Regulatory Considerations**: Certain nonprofits (healthcare, education, social services) may have additional compliance requirements for their communications.

5. **Volunteer Implementation**: Often, volunteers with varying technical skills will be implementing or modifying these designs, requiring user-friendly approaches.

6. **Emotional Connection**: Nonprofit visuals must balance emotional appeal with respectful representation, avoiding exploitation while still creating impact.

7. **Multi-Channel Usage**: Limited resources mean each visual needs to work effectively across multiple platforms (social media, website, email, print) with minimal customization.

8. **Measurement Requirements**: Nonprofits need to demonstrate communication effectiveness to funders, requiring designs that support clear impact measurement.
</CONTEXT>

### Example #1: Community Food Bank Fundraising Dinner

**Input:**
We need promotional images for our annual "Harvest Hope Dinner" fundraiser. The event raises money for our food bank that serves 5,000 families monthly. Our colors are green and orange, and we want to emphasize community and abundance rather than hunger. The event includes a silent auction and local chef showcase. Target audience is primarily local business leaders and individual donors aged 40-65.

**Thoughts:**
This event needs to balance celebration with purpose. The visuals should evoke abundance and community while subtly reminding viewers of the mission. The target audience suggests a more sophisticated approach, but it should remain warm and accessible. The organization's green and orange colors can be incorporated through food imagery (harvest vegetables) and natural elements.

**Output:**

## 1. VISUAL STRATEGY OVERVIEW
- Core Message Distillation: "Community coming together to celebrate abundance and share it with neighbors in need"
- Target Audience Analysis: Business professionals and established donors who respond to both emotional appeals and demonstrations of impact
- Brand Alignment Strategy: Using the organization's green and orange colors as inspiration for a harvest theme that suggests abundance rather than scarcity
- Distribution Channel Optimization: Primary focus on email invitations and social media announcements, with adaptations for printed programs

## 2. CONCEPT DIRECTIONS

### CONCEPT A: "Community Table"
**Design Approach:** Overhead view of a beautifully set table with multiple hands reaching in to share food
**Emotional Response:** Warmth, community, generosity
**Key Visual Elements:**
- Rustic wooden table with elegant but approachable place settings
- Diverse hands passing plates and serving food
- Warm lighting suggesting evening dinner atmosphere
- Subtle integration of harvest vegetables in organization colors

**DALL-E PROMPT:**
```
Create a warm, inviting promotional image for a nonprofit fundraising dinner from an overhead perspective. The design should feature a rustic wooden table with elegant place settings and multiple diverse hands sharing and passing plates of food. Use a color palette of warm oranges, deep greens, and soft golden lighting that evokes community and abundance. The overall mood should be generous and hopeful. Include subtle harvest vegetables (orange squash, green herbs) arranged naturally on the table. The image should be landscape orientation with a softly blurred background suggesting an elegant but welcoming venue.
```

**Design Specifications:**
- Primary Colors: Deep green (#2C5E1A), Warm orange (#E67E22), Complementary gold (#D4AC0D)
- Typography Recommendations: Serif fonts for headings (Garamond), sans-serif for details (Open Sans)
- Layout Structure: Central image with text overlay in upper third and lower third
- Text Placement Guide: Event name at top, date/time/location at bottom, logo in lower right corner

**Adaptation Guidelines:**
- Social Media Version: Crop to square format, enlarge text slightly, add subtle motion effect
- Email Version: Ensure text is part of image and repeated in email body for accessibility
- Print Version: Extend background color to edges for bleed, increase resolution to 300dpi
